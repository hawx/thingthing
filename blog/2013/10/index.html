Esendex at HackManchester 2013

It was a fragile, subdued development team at the Esendex office this morning. That’s because this weekend 13 of us attended HackManchester, a 24 hour coding competition at the Museum of Science and Industry in Manchester.

From 14:00 on Saturday until 14:00 Sunday 230 developers in 72 teams competed to submit the best entry to any of the five sponsors’ programming challenges. At the end, each team made a video showing off their hard work.

Esendex entered three teams. Here are their entries:

QuizBang – An SMS-based multiplayer trivia elimination game

 

HackTrack – A team planning and activity tracking tool for hack events

 

TeamSauce – A tool for hack events that helps organisers and sponsors communicate with the teams (challenge finalist!)

 

Nottingham was well represented at the event. In addition to Esendex’s teams, the University of Nottingham HackSoc were there, entering some brilliant hacks and winning some well-deserved prizes.

Our intrepid team of hackers

Well done to all the teams that entered. We were astounded with what can be achieved in just 24 hours of coding. A big thank you to Gemma Cameron, Joseph Swann and Claire Foster for organising the event. We had enormous fun, and came away inspired but very sleepy.

Get ready for Hack Manchester 2013!

After the huge success of last year, it’s great to see that Hack Manchester is taking place again, for the second year running!

It will be held at the same place – the magnificent Museum of Science and Industry in the heart of Manchester and is on the 26th and 27th of October 2013.

Hack Manchester 2013 is a 24-hour coding competition and the the idea is that teams of up to four turn up with an idea and just 24 hours later present a working product.

Last year we helped to sponsor the event and we set a challenge for teams to come up with the best and most ingenious use of our API. The winners were a team from 7digital – they created an online application that allows you to organise football games between you and your friends using text messages. Take a look at our Hack Manchester write-up for more information.

This year several of our developers and even our web designer will be getting involved and we’ll be entering 3 teams to tackle some of the exciting challenges on offer.

Keep an eye on our blog for the event write-up and as we’ll be tweeting live, follow us @esendexdev.

It’s not too late to get involved but tickets are selling fast – to grab yours visit hackmanchester.eventbrite.co.uk.
